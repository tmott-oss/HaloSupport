# Halosight Master Context

## Company Overview

Halosight is an AI-powered revenue intelligence platform designed to capture, structure, and activate customer interaction data across sales teams.

It integrates with systems such as Salesforce and communication tools to:

- capture live and recorded sales conversations
- convert unstructured interactions into structured CRM data
- provide real-time and post-meeting guidance to sales reps
- enable leadership visibility into account-level activity and deal progression

Core problem solved:

Revenue teams often operate with incomplete, inconsistent, or missing customer interaction data. That creates poor forecasting, weak coaching, limited account visibility, and avoidable revenue loss.

## ICP

### Company-Level Criteria

- mid-market to enterprise organizations
- 100+ sales reps or complex field-sales motion
- industrial distribution, manufacturing, field-sales-heavy organizations, or B2B services with long sales cycles

- experiencing poor CRM data quality, inconsistent sales process execution, weak visibility into rep activity, or revenue unpredictability

## Objection Handling Framework

### "We already use Salesforce / Gong / Chorus"

- those tools store or analyze data
- Halosight ensures the data exists and is structured properly

### "Reps will not use it"

- reps do not need to actively use Halosight
- it runs in the background and removes work rather than adding it

### "Security concerns"

- position Halosight as aligned with enterprise data governance
- mention certifications only when verified by authoritative internal sources

### "This feels like monitoring reps"

- frame this around improving outcomes, not surveillance
- focus on helping reps win more deals with less admin work

### "We already have a process"

- Halosight does not replace process
- it validates and reinforces execution of the existing process

## Competitive Positioning

## Conversation Tree

Primary states:

1. opening
2. problem validation
3. impact quantification
4. solution alignment
5. objection handling
6. commitment / next step
7. exit / disqualify

### Opening

Objective:

Earn 30 to 60 seconds of attention.

Pattern:

"Most teams we talk to are not struggling with effort. They are struggling because customer interactions never make it into the CRM in a usable way."

Advance when:

- the prospect acknowledges the issue
- the prospect engages with a relevant follow-up question

### Problem Validation

Objective:

Confirm the pain is real and current.

Core questions:

- how are reps capturing customer interactions today
- how reliable is that data inside Salesforce
- if ten accounts were reviewed, would the notes reflect reality

Advance when:

- the prospect admits inconsistency, gaps, or lack of trust

### Impact Quantification

Objective:

Tie the problem to revenue loss and operating risk.

Core questions:

- how does this affect forecast accuracy
- how confident are you in what is really happening in deals
- what happens when a top rep leaves

Advance when:

- the prospect acknowledges business impact, not just inconvenience

### Solution Alignment

Objective:

Map Halosight to the stated problem without pitching features in isolation.

Pattern:

"What you described, missing and inconsistent interaction data, is exactly what Halosight fixes. We capture those interactions automatically and turn them into structured CRM data your team can use."

Advance when:

- the prospect shows alignment or requests the next step
- if hesitation appears, route into objection handling

### Commitment / Next Step

Objective:

Advance the deal with a specific next step.

Examples:

- map this to the prospect's Salesforce environment
- run it against a defined subset of the team

### Exit / Disqualify

Use when:

- there is no pain
- there is no urgency
- the account does not fit the ICP
