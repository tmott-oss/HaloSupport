# Halosight Qualification Rules

## Strong fit

Strong fit if most of the following are true:

- field or outside sales motion exists
- high-touch account interactions matter
- Salesforce or similar CRM is central
- CRM hygiene is weak or inconsistent
- leadership lacks visibility into real customer interactions
- manager coaching depends on incomplete rep updates
- commitments, opportunities, or customer signals are often lost
- organization is willing to adopt phased rollout
- rep admin burden is a recognized issue

## Moderate fit

- mostly inside sales but still weak note capture / CRM execution
- leadership visibility problems exist
- follow-up discipline is inconsistent
- there is appetite for workflow automation

## Weak fit

- low-touch transactional sales with little human interaction
- no meaningful CRM process
- no concern for data quality or manager visibility
- no field motion and no customer interaction blind spot
- organization only wants generic AI novelty

## Disqualify / red flags

- prospect wants unsupported compliance/security commitments
- no system-of-record discipline at all
- no executive sponsor
- no willingness to change rep workflow
- no meaningful pain beyond “AI sounds interesting”
- pure feature-shopping against Gong/Clari without a field data problem
- expects immediate full-platform transformation without phased adoption
- rep culture strongly rejects any capture tool and leadership will not support process change

## Qualification guidance

Agents should not confuse engagement with qualification. The account should only advance if:

- the pain is explicit
- the operational consequences are clear
- a relevant persona cares enough to act
- Halosight’s use cases clearly map to the problem
- next-step motion is tied to a concrete buying process
