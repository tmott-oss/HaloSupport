# Halosight Sales Process Definitions

Use these definitions unless Halosight publishes a formal alternative.

## Awareness

The prospect recognizes a symptom or inefficiency but has not fully defined the root problem or business impact.

### Signals

- curiosity
- acknowledges pain
- asks broad “how it works” questions
- no agreed initiative yet

## Problem-Defined

The prospect agrees there is a real operational problem and can describe it in business and process terms.

### Signals

- names current-state failure
- acknowledges impact on reps, managers, data, or forecast
- confirms current workflow gaps
- internal pain is explicit

## Business Case

The prospect connects the problem to business outcomes and sees value in change.

### Signals

- discusses cost of inaction
- discusses time savings, data quality, visibility, or revenue implications
- multiple stakeholders care
- priority begins to form

## Solution Fit

The prospect believes Halosight’s approach maps credibly to their problem.

### Signals

- validates use cases
- explores implementation
- asks integration, security, or admin questions
- wants workflow walkthroughs or phased plan

## Decision

The buying group is evaluating concrete next steps toward purchase.

### Signals

- procurement, security, legal, or SOW motion
- implementation planning
- pricing review
- stakeholder alignment effort
- success criteria discussed
