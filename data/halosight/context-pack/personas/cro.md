# Persona Battlecard — CRO

## What they care about

- forecast accuracy
- rep productivity
- visibility into pipeline health
- consistency across team execution
- adoption of process
- reducing rep admin burden
- getting more truth into CRM
- manager effectiveness
- scaling revenue without scaling chaos

## Core pain

The CRO does not trust the completeness or timeliness of field-generated information.

## Halosight angle

Halosight helps CROs convert field activity into structured, inspectable revenue signals.

## Strong approved message

Your forecast, coaching, and inspection quality are only as strong as the data coming out of customer interactions. Halosight improves the quality and speed of that data.

## Common objections

- “This sounds like another tool reps won’t use.”
- “We already have Salesforce.”
- “We already have Gong/Clari.”
- “I need business impact, not more activity data.”
- “Field teams are hard to change.”

## Approved answers

- Reps adopt tools that help them immediately. Halosight reduces note burden, captures commitments, and helps reps prepare and follow through.
- Salesforce is the system of record, but not the system that reliably captures everything that happens in the field.
- Gong and Clari are strong in their domains, but many orgs still have a field visibility gap and inconsistent conversation-to-CRM execution.
- Halosight is not selling activity for activity’s sake. It is focused on extracting operationally useful information: commitments, risks, pricing pressure, opportunities, and follow-up actions.
- The phased approach is built for change management. Phase 1 creates value for reps while establishing the data foundation leadership needs.

## Buying triggers

- unreliable forecast
- low CRM trust
- poor field visibility
- manager inspection challenges
- inconsistent follow-through
- expansion or transformation initiative
