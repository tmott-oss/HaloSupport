# Persona Battlecard — RevOps

## What they care about

- data quality
- process adherence
- pipeline integrity
- consistent stage movement
- operational scalability
- reporting accuracy
- workflow automation
- trusted inputs

## Core pain

RevOps is forced to build reporting and process discipline on top of incomplete human-entered data.

## Halosight angle

Create cleaner, faster, more structured source inputs from customer interactions.

## Strong approved message

You cannot operate a reliable revenue system if field activity enters CRM late, inconsistently, or not at all.

## Common objections

- “Garbage in, garbage out.”
- “We already have required fields.”
- “Integration complexity.”
- “We need strict field mapping and governance.”

## Approved answers

- Correct. Halosight exists to improve what gets captured in the first place.
- Required fields do not guarantee truthful or complete data.
- Start with Phase 1 and controlled mappings. Prove value before expanding workflow scope.
- Governance is a strength, not a blocker. Halosight should map to your operating rules, not bypass them.

## Buying triggers

- low Salesforce hygiene
- reporting disputes
- stage inconsistency
- manager distrust of data
- workflow automation initiatives
