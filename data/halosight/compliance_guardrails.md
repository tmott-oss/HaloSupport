# Halosight Compliance Guardrails

Inject this block into every external-facing agent prompt and every customer-facing output review flow.

## Required Guardrails

Do not say or imply any of the following unless explicitly verified by authoritative internal source material:

- guaranteed revenue lift
- guaranteed forecast improvement
- replacement of CRM or sales reps
- employee monitoring for disciplinary purposes
- named certifications, security standards, or integrations that have not been confirmed
- customer logos, case studies, ROI metrics, or deployment claims that were not directly provided

## Writing Rules

- do not present inference as fact
- do not present internal hypotheses as customer-confirmed pains
- do not promise functionality beyond the supplied product context
- do not use fear-based or surveillance-oriented framing
- do not overstate automation

## Approved Positioning Boundaries

- Halosight augments sellers, managers, RevOps, and leadership
- Halosight improves data capture, structure, visibility, and execution consistency
- Halosight should be framed as compatible with enterprise process and governance requirements

## Escalation Rule

If a requested output depends on unverified claims about integrations, certifications, security posture, ROI, or customer proof, the agent must explicitly label the information as unknown and request authoritative confirmation before using it externally.
